80C/80CCC/80CCD(1) Deduction(Investments)

What is Section 80C Deduction?

Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961, allows you to reduce your taxable income by a certain amount if you invest or spend in specified financial instruments and expenses. This reduction in taxable income results in lower income tax liability. They not only help you with saving taxes but also help you grow your money.

Maximum Deduction Limit

The maximum deduction allowed under Section 80C is Rs 1.5 lakh in a financial year. This means you can reduce your taxable income by up to Rs 1.5 lakh if you make investments or incur expenses that qualify for this deduction

How it Works?

Let's say your total taxable income for the year is Rs 6,00,000, and you've invested Rs 1,50,000 in PPF and paid Rs 50,000 as a life insurance premium during that year. You can deduct the total of these investments and premiums (Rs 1,50,000 + Rs 50,000 = Rs 2,00,000) from your taxable income. Your taxable income would then be reduced from Rs 6,00,000 to Rs 4,00,000. This lower taxable income will determine how much income tax you owe for the year.

Benefits

  • Lower Tax Liability: Section 80C helps in reducing your taxable income, which, in turn, lowers the amount of income tax you need to pay.
  • Long-term Savings: It encourages individuals to save and invest for long-term financial goals like retirement, education, and homeownership.
  • Financial Security: Investments in instruments like PPF and life insurance provide financial security to individuals and their families

    • Documentation

    Keep proper documentation of your investments and expenses, such as receipts and certificates, to claim the deduction accurately when filing your income tax return.
